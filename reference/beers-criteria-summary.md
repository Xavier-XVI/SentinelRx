# Beers Criteria — OTC-Relevant Summary (AGS 2023)

Source: American Geriatrics Society Beers Criteria® 2023 Update. J Am Geriatr Soc. 2023.
Scope: OTC-applicable drug classes only. Full criteria includes prescription-only entries not listed here.

---

## Class I — Avoid in All Older Adults (65+)

| Drug / Class | OTC Products | Primary Risk | Alternative |
|---|---|---|---|
| First-gen antihistamines (diphenhydramine, chlorpheniramine, doxylamine) | Benadryl, ZzzQuil, Unisom, Tylenol PM, NyQuil, any "PM" product | Confusion, delirium, fall risk, urinary retention, sedation | Loratadine (Claritin), cetirizine (Zyrtec) — non-anticholinergic |
| NSAIDs — oral (ibuprofen, naproxen, aspirin >325mg/day) | Advil, Aleve, Motrin IB, Excedrin | GI bleeding/ulcer, renal failure, HTN, CHF exacerbation, fluid retention | Acetaminophen (Tylenol) |
| Mineral oil (oral) | Fleet Mineral Oil | Aspiration pneumonia | Docusate, polyethylene glycol |

---

## Class II — Avoid Unless No Safer Alternative (65+)

| Drug / Class | OTC Products | Primary Risk | Condition |
|---|---|---|---|
| Aspirin (>325mg/day for pain) | Excedrin, BC Powder | GI bleeding, especially with anticoagulants | History of GI bleed or anticoagulant use |
| Pseudoephedrine / phenylephrine | Sudafed, Sudafed PE, DayQuil, Claritin-D | HTN, cardiac arrhythmia, urinary retention | Hypertension, benign prostatic hyperplasia |
| Stimulant laxatives (chronic use) | Ex-Lax, Dulcolax (bisacodyl) | Dependence, electrolyte imbalance | Use only for opioid-induced constipation |

## Drug–Disease Interactions (Beers Criteria — OTC Relevant)

| OTC Drug Class | Condition to Check | Risk |
|---|---|---|
| NSAIDs | CKD (any stage) | Acute kidney injury; accelerate CKD progression |
| NSAIDs | Heart failure | Fluid retention; CHF exacerbation |
| NSAIDs | Hypertension | Reduce antihypertensive efficacy; raise BP |
| NSAIDs | GI history (ulcer/bleed) | GI hemorrhage |
| NSAIDs | Anticoagulant use | Major bleeding |
| Decongestants | Hypertension | BP elevation; arrhythmia |
| Decongestants | Benign prostatic hyperplasia | Urinary retention |
| First-gen antihistamines | Dementia / cognitive impairment | Acute delirium; accelerate cognitive decline |
| First-gen antihistamines | Constipation | Worsen constipation (anticholinergic effect) |
| First-gen antihistamines | Fall risk (any) | High fall and fracture risk |

---

## Anticholinergic Burden Note

Older adults accumulate anticholinergic burden across multiple medications. A patient on a prescription anticholinergic (bladder, GI, or psych) who adds an OTC antihistamine (diphenhydramine) compounds risk — even if each drug alone is "low dose." Always assess total anticholinergic load when a caregiver proposes adding any OTC with anticholinergic properties.
