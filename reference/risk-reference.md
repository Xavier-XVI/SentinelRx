# SentinelRx — Risk Reference

Two sections:
1. OTC drug class × prescription drug class interaction matrix
2. OTC products commonly assumed safe — flagged for elderly

---

## Section 1 — Interaction Matrix

Severity codes: **MAJOR** (DO NOT TAKE) | **MODERATE** (CAUTION + monitoring) | **MINOR** (note only) | **TIMING** (timing adjustment resolves risk)

| OTC Drug / Class | Rx Drug / Class | Severity | Mechanism | Required action |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| NSAIDs (ibuprofen, naproxen) | Warfarin / anticoagulants | MAJOR | Antiplatelet + anticoagulant = bleeding; warfarin INR destabilized | DO NOT TAKE |
| NSAIDs | Apixaban, rivaroxaban, edoxaban | MAJOR | Additive bleeding risk; no antidote for NOACs if bleed occurs | DO NOT TAKE |
| NSAIDs | ACE inhibitors (lisinopril, enalapril) | MODERATE | NSAIDs reduce ACE inhibitor antihypertensive effect; increase renal vasoconstriction | CAUTION — monitor BP and renal function |
| NSAIDs | ARBs (losartan, valsartan) | MODERATE | Same mechanism as ACE inhibitor interaction | CAUTION |
| NSAIDs | Diuretics (furosemide, HCTZ) | MODERATE | NSAIDs blunt diuretic response; fluid retention, BP rise | CAUTION |
| NSAIDs | Digoxin | MODERATE | NSAIDs reduce renal clearance of digoxin → toxicity risk | CAUTION — monitor digoxin levels |
| NSAIDs | Metformin | MODERATE | NSAIDs reduce renal blood flow; metformin accumulation → lactic acidosis risk in CKD | CAUTION — especially in CKD |
| NSAIDs | Corticosteroids (prednisolone, etc.) | MAJOR | Synergistic GI ulcer/bleed risk | DO NOT TAKE |
| Aspirin >325mg/day | Warfarin | MAJOR | As above; aspirin adds antiplatelet effect | DO NOT TAKE |
| Aspirin (any dose) | Other NSAIDs | MODERATE | Aspirin blocks cardioprotective effect of ibuprofen; additive GI risk | CAUTION — separate doses |
| Diphenhydramine | Any anticholinergic Rx | MAJOR | Cumulative anticholinergic burden → acute delirium, urinary retention, constipation | DO NOT TAKE |
| Diphenhydramine | Benzodiazepines / sedatives | MAJOR | Additive CNS depression; fall and respiratory risk | DO NOT TAKE |
| Doxylamine | Same as diphenhydramine | MAJOR | Same anticholinergic + CNS mechanism | DO NOT TAKE |
| DXM (dextromethorphan) | SSRIs / SNRIs (sertraline, fluoxetine, venlafaxine) | MAJOR | Serotonin syndrome — agitation, hyperthermia, tremor, tachycardia | DO NOT TAKE |
| DXM | MAOIs (phenelzine, tranylcypromine) | MAJOR | Severe serotonin syndrome; potentially fatal | DO NOT TAKE — 14-day MAOI washout required |
| DXM | Tramadol | MODERATE | Serotonin syndrome; also additive CNS depression | CAUTION — CALL PHARMACIST |
| Pseudoephedrine | Antihypertensives (any class) | MAJOR | Adrenergic stimulation raises BP; counteracts antihypertensive | DO NOT TAKE |
| Phenylephrine | Antihypertensives | MODERATE | Less potent than pseudoephedrine but same mechanism | DO NOT TAKE in poorly controlled HTN; CAUTION otherwise |
| Calcium carbonate (antacid) | Levothyroxine | TIMING | Calcium chelates thyroid hormone in GI tract → reduced absorption | CAUTION — 4h separation required |
| Calcium carbonate | Alendronate / bisphosphonates | TIMING | Same chelation mechanism | CAUTION — take bisphosphonate first; antacid later |
| Calcium carbonate | Digoxin | MODERATE | Reduces digoxin absorption unpredictably | CALL PHARMACIST |
| Calcium carbonate | Fluoroquinolone antibiotics | TIMING | Reduces antibiotic absorption | CAUTION — 2h separation |
| Magnesium hydroxide (antacid) | Digoxin | MODERATE | Reduces digoxin absorption | CALL PHARMACIST |
| PPIs (omeprazole, famotidine) | Clopidogrel | MODERATE | PPIs reduce clopidogrel activation (CYP2C19 inhibition) | CAUTION — if patient is on clopidogrel, CALL PHARMACIST |
| Acetaminophen (>3,000mg/day) | Any hepatotoxic Rx | MODERATE | Additive liver stress | CAUTION — calculate total daily dose including all products |
| Fish oil (>1,000mg/day) | Warfarin / NOACs | MAJOR | Omega-3 fatty acids inhibit platelet aggregation; elevate INR | DO NOT TAKE without physician review |
| Fish oil | Aspirin | MODERATE | Additive antiplatelet effect | CAUTION |
| Turmeric / curcumin (high dose) | Warfarin / NOACs | MAJOR | Anticoagulant effect; INR destabilization | DO NOT TAKE |
| Ginkgo biloba | Warfarin / NOACs | MAJOR | Platelet inhibition + anticoagulant = bleeding risk | DO NOT TAKE |
| Ginkgo biloba | SSRIs | MODERATE | Serotonin modulation + bleeding risk | CAUTION — CALL PHARMACIST |
| Garlic extract (high dose) | Warfarin | MAJOR | Anticoagulant effect | DO NOT TAKE |
| St. John's Wort | Any antidepressant | MAJOR | Serotonin syndrome | DO NOT TAKE |
| St. John's Wort | Digoxin | MAJOR | CYP450 induction → reduces digoxin to subtherapeutic levels | DO NOT TAKE |
| St. John's Wort | Warfarin | MAJOR | CYP450 induction → reduces warfarin → clot risk | DO NOT TAKE |
| St. John's Wort | Oral contraceptives | MAJOR | Reduces efficacy (lower relevance in 65+ but noted) | DO NOT TAKE |
| Melatonin | Benzodiazepines / sedatives | MODERATE | Additive sedation; fall risk | CAUTION |
| Melatonin | Warfarin | MINOR | Minor anticoagulant potentiation at high doses | Note only at standard doses (0.5–3mg) |
| Vitamin E >400 IU/day | Warfarin | MODERATE | Anticoagulant potentiation | CAUTION — flag if patient's total Vitamin E load is high |

## Section 2 — OTC Products Commonly Assumed Safe — Flagged for Elderly

| Product | Common assumption | Actual risk for 65+ |
|---|---|---|
| Tylenol PM / Advil PM / Aleve PM | "It's just Tylenol/Advil with sleep" | Contains diphenhydramine — Beers Class I. DO NOT TAKE for 65+. |
| Benadryl | "It's just an allergy pill" | Diphenhydramine — Beers Class I. Confusion, falls, retention. DO NOT TAKE. |
| ZzzQuil / Unisom SleepTabs | "It's a natural sleep aid" | Diphenhydramine or doxylamine — Beers Class I. DO NOT TAKE. |
| NyQuil | "It's just cold medicine" | Contains doxylamine (anticholinergic) + DXM + acetaminophen. Multiple interaction risks. |
| Aleve / naproxen | "It lasts longer so you need fewer doses" | NSAID — 12h half-life means prolonged renal and GI exposure. Avoid in 65+. |
| Fish oil | "Heart-healthy, natural" | Anticoagulant at high doses. Major interaction with warfarin/NOACs. |
| Melatonin 5–10mg | "It's natural, can't hurt" | Standard adult doses are 5–10x what elderly need. Sedation risk at high doses. |
| Excedrin | "Just a headache pill" | Acetaminophen + aspirin (NSAID) + caffeine. Triple interaction risk on complex profiles. |
| Sudafed / Sudafed PE | "It's just a decongestant" | Raises blood pressure. Contraindicated with antihypertensives. |
| Throat lozenges with benzocaine | Low-risk assumed | Generally safe, but high doses of benzocaine can cause methemoglobinemia in frail elderly |
